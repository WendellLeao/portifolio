About me

I have been working professionally as a game developer for over two years using the Unity and C# graphics engine. I am currently working with a team of Brazilian developers on the game Rogue Masters, acting as a networking programmer.

Rogue Masters

"Rogue Masters is a cooperative game of up to 6 players with souls-like style combat."

That's the project I'm currently working on. I work mainly in the networking part, I am responsible for the synchronization of the gameplay mechanics and for the development and optimization in the infrastructure of the project code.

Where are you?

"Where Are You is a cooperative horror game with support of up to 4 players. Your goal is to investigate haunted houses and exorcise the ghost that haunts them."

I'm developing this project myself and plan to release it on Steam one day. I take care of all the programming and game design on my own. I use ready-made assets to compose the graphical part of the game.

Origens

"Origens is a 2D platform game where the main character takes a journey in a newly created world that evolves geologically through the ages."

This project was the Final Paper of my graduation of Technologist in Digital Games. I worked in it as a programmer, artist, screenwriter and game designer.

Arrow is Cool in the Cemetery Skull

"You're in a cemetery and have to shoot arrows at hordes of skeletons to survive as long as possible."

This project was a programming test. The goal was to develop a project with as much technical quality as possible to assess how much I learned during my bootcamping period. I just took care of the programming part.

Overthrow 2

"In a dream-themed game, you have to knock down the obstacles in your way to get to the end."

I developed this game during my bootcamp period. I had the help of senior programmers who accompanied me and taught me a lot about programming. I did the whole game myself.

The Amazing Claudio

"Shoot enemies to advance and face the big boss in this cartoon-style 2D platformer."

A simple personal project I did during the holidays to practice programming. I took care of everything except the art that was obtained from a free asset store.

Fruits

"Face the enemies and collect all the fruits to advance to the next level in this beautiful 2D platformer."

Another personal project that I developed to practice. My main goal with this project was to develop a simple game with fun and fluid mechanics. The art was purchased in a free asset store.

Jump Up 2

"Jump the obstacles and beat the platform challenges to advance to the next stage."

This project is a remake of the first game I've ever developed. The goal was to compare how much I evolved as a programmer in a one-year period of studies. I did the whole game myself.
